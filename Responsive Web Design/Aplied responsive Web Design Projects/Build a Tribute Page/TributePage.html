Thomas Alva Edison

American inventor and businessman

Photograph of Edison with his phonograph (2nd model), taken in Mathew Brady's Washington, DC studio in April 1878.

Here's a time line of Thomas Edisons's life:

  • 1847 - Born in Milan, Ohio
  • 1866 - at the age of 19, Edison moved to Louisville, Kentucky, where, as an employee of Western Union, he worked the Associated Press bureau news wire
  • 1871 - Edison married 16-year-old Mary Stilwell (1855 - 1884), whom he had met two months earlier
  • 1877 - Edison began his career as an inventor in Newark, New Jersey, with the automatic repeater and his other improved telegraphic devices, but the invention that first gained him notice was the phonograph
  • 1879 - The first successful test of lightbulb lasted 13.5 hours
  • 1879 - Edison made the first public demonstration of his incandescent light bulb in Menlo Park
  • 1891 - Thomas Edison built a Kinetoscope or peep-hole viewer
